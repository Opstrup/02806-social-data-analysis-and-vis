Part 1: Telling Data Stories

  • What is the Oxford English Dictionary's defintion of a narrative?

    • The word ‘narrative’ as a noun is defined as a story. It is a written or spoken account of connected events.

  • What is your favorite visualization among the examples in section 3? Explain why in a few words.

    • Our favorite visualization is the one in Fig.1, ‘Steroids Or Not, the Pursuit is On’, from New York Times. The visualization instantly catches the attention of the reader with the big main graphs going across the visualization and the main headline ‘755’ pointed to by the image of Bonds. The minimalist design uses the same color scheme as the images, which makes it pleasant and easy to read and understand.

    The cumulative homerun graph in the middle, is easy to understand without any knowledge about Baseball, while the bar charts in the bottom provides insight into when the players in question hit their prime, in terms of amounts of homeruns per season.

    The visualization manages to tell an interesting story by guiding the reader through the data using visual highlighting, such as the color, size and boldness of the different sections. The small annotations are very concise and provide valuable extra information that strengthens the narrative told by the visualization.

    • What is your favorite genre of narrative visualization? Why?

      • Our favorite genre of narrative visualization is the partitioned poster, because the combination of informative images and graphs with explaining annotations and captions is able to convey a lot of information in a very interesting way and using only a very small amount of space.

    • What is your least favorite genre? Why?

      • Our least favorite genre of narrative visualization is the slide show, because the ability to add multiple slides often results in very inefficient visual narratives, where a lot of unnecessary information is added because the author isn't forced to choose the most informative data to display.

      When the information is spread across multiple slides it might be hard to get a good overview of the data and follow the narrative being told.
